Project Proposal

Summary

Video games created in the early and mid 2000s were pioneering games that sparked interest in many enthustiast's around the world, giving birth to the game industry as we know it. These video games pushed forth the desire for realistic graphics, open world maps, and advanced animations. With the incline in graphic simulations, people tend to look back at the classics and wonder what their cherished childhood games would looklike if realsed today. With that purpose, our team plans to integrate high definition graphics/ textures, and advanced lighting to one of our childhood favorites. Our team consists of James Razo, Jesus Cebreros, Kevin Merino, and Noe Cebreros.

Problem Description

Most older games were limited to the technology of their day. Due to the lack of mainstream heavy computational resources most games shipped with low quality graphics, minimial textures, and had to sacrifice computational hungry tasks such as ray tracing. Such games include Left 4 Dead, Half Life, Portal, and The Sims. Our team seeks to improve the graphics of one of these four games by introducing lighting techniques from CS184 (raytracing), reproducing some texture layouts in HD, and possibly modifying the game engine for supersampling. We will accomplish this through the use of shading tools or by creating new textures and updating them according to the game scenery. We see an example image below of a scenery that could be improved by introducing material modeling to the vehicle, possibly higher count raytracing, ambient occlusion, and higher definition textures.

It is important to us that we generate new graphics for one of these games because we grew up playing them and we would like to breathe a new life into them via updated graphics. The challenges with this goal will be acquiring the correct tools to start modding one of these games; learning how to use blender, or any other tool to update texture maps. Also, learning how to modify the pre-existing rendering pipeline to introduce our own shading techniques and lighting effects. We forsee video game ray tracing as specially difficult not only because this is completely new to us but because it is a rather computational hungry task to accomplish in real time. However, we believe through organization and maticulous planning we can introduce the above tasks into one our favorite video games. We would also like to have our game be playable, however, we would at least like to be able to render in-game footage that will showcase our upgrades.

Goals and Deliverables

Since we have access to the original rendering's game scenery, we will be able to showcase our improved quality graphics via a side by side image comparison of before and after environments. Our goal is to deliver footage of the game through a cinematic walkthrough of different scenes that portray our modifications for a thrilling throwback experience. The quality should be measured through the accuracy of the images, video framerate and texture pixel count. Our side by side comparison will ultimately answer whether our new modified scenery is a dramatic improvement from the original scenery. We plan on showcasing what modern technology allows us todo to older game graphics. We expect to produce a partially modified game with breathtaking new scenery, reconstruct in high quality the more visually appealing textures and inject new shaders. We expect to be unable to completely modify all game graphics in the allotted time period but we hope to target the game components that result in the most dramatic and appealing results. In order to accomplish this we plan to borrow techniques from shader mods used in minecraft, learn how to use blender to create better visuals, and modify the existing pipeline to integrate everything. We also intend on implementing or modifiying ray tracing to produce game scenes that look as realistic as possible. If everything goes according to plan, we hope to deliver an in-game footage with path-traced HD texture scenes. If we are able to get ahead of schedule, we plan on implementing real-time ray tracing on the GPU to achieve a fully playable remastered videogame.
